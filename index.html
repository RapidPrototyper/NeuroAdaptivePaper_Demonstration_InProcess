Neuroadaptive Cursor Experiment

Based on the paper "Neuroadaptive technology enables implicit cursor control based on medial prefrontal cortex activity" (Zander et al., 2016) - Demonstrated by Muhammad Hinan Khan

Experiment Structure:

  1. Calibration Phase: Watch the robot move randomly. No button presses needed. Just observe movements with automatic breaks.
  2. BCI Phase: Robot adapts based on simulated brain signals. No button presses needed. Watch as the robot becomes smarter.
  3. Manual Phase: ACTIVE PARTICIPATION REQUIRED - Press V if movement was acceptable (toward target), B if not acceptable.
Important: You only need to press keys during the Manual Phase. Other phases are observation only.
